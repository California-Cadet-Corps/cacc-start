Who We Are

About the California Cadet Corps

The program is organized around five core strands, each designed to build a well-rounded cadet ready for any career or path after high school.

🏆

Leadership

Develop decision-making, command presence, and the ability to lead peers in high-stress situations.

🎯

Citizenship

Foster civic awareness, community service, and a sense of duty to California and the nation.

👑

Military Subjects

Learn drill & ceremonies, land navigation, marksmanship safety, and military courtesies.

🏃

Healthy Living

Build physical fitness, nutrition habits, and mental resilience through PT and wellness programs.

📚

Academics

Reinforce classroom skills — goal-setting, study habits, and academic achievement count toward advancement.

Promotion Path

Your First Four Ranks

Every cadet starts as a Recruit and earns their way up. Advancement requires Time-in-Grade (TIG) and a Performance Task Assessment (PTA). There is no automatic right to promotion — it is earned.

  1. RCT

    Recruit

    First Semester — Day One

    Every cadet begins here. As a Recruit you are learning the ropes — uniform standards, cadet courtesies, basic drill, and the culture of the corps. No PTA is required to enter; your clock starts ticking the day you join your unit.

  2. CDT

    Cadet

    ~After 1 Semester + PTA

    After roughly one semester of satisfactory participation you become eligible for your first PTA. Pass it and you receive the Cadet rank — the first real milestone. Attendance, attitude, and effort all factor in.

  3. CFC

    Cadet First Class

    ~After 2nd Semester + PTA

    With a second semester of quality service and another PTA, you advance to Cadet First Class. You are no longer the newest person in the room and start taking on junior mentorship responsibilities.

  4. C/CPL

    Cadet Corporal

    ~After 3rd Semester + PTA

    Corporal is the first Non-Commissioned Officer (NCO) rank. You now lead small groups, set the standard for junior cadets, and are expected to know California Cadet Corps regulations and your unit's mission cold. A significant milestone — and just the beginning of the NCO path.

Authoritative promotion criteria: cacadets.org/Cadet/Rank Structure and CR 1-5 (Cadet Regulation 1-5 — Promotions). Requirements listed here are guidance; official criteria govern.

Awards

Earn Your First Ribbons

Ribbons are worn on your dress uniform and represent achievement. As a new cadet, these seven are the most accessible — focus here first.

Perfect Attendance

Attend every scheduled unit meeting during a grading period without an unexcused absence.

Physical Fitness

Meet or exceed the Army Physical Fitness Test (APFT) standards for your age and gender.

Marksmanship Training

Complete the required marksmanship safety training and qualify on the range or EST.

Basic Leadership School

Attend and successfully complete a California Cadet Corps-sponsored Basic Leadership School (BLS).

Bivouac

Participate in a sanctioned overnight bivouac field training exercise with your unit.

Individual Community Service

Complete the minimum required hours of individual community service outside of cadet events.

Citizenship

Demonstrate exemplary citizenship contributions as assessed by your unit commander.

Full ribbon criteria: cacadets.org/Cadet/Ribbon Chart and CR 1-1 (Cadet Regulation 1-1 — Awards). Specific requirements may vary; consult your unit S1 or commander.

Getting Started

What a New Cadet Needs

Your first weeks set the tone. Check off these five priorities and you will be ahead of the curve before your first PTA.

  • 1
    Learn Cadet Courtesies Know when and how to salute, how to address officers and NCOs, and the basic customs of the corps. First impressions are lasting ones.
  • 2
    Get and Maintain Your Uniform Your unit will issue your uniform — learn how to wear it correctly. Inspect it before every meeting: pressed, boots shined, insignia straight. A sharp uniform signals you take this seriously.
  • 3
    Attend Every Meeting Attendance is the single biggest factor in early promotion and ribbon eligibility. Missing meetings without an excused absence stalls your clock. Show up, on time, in uniform, every time.
  • 4
    Set Your First Promotion Goal: RCT → CDT Talk to your NCO or commander about what the PTA looks like for your unit. Know what is expected, start practicing now, and ask for feedback early.
  • 5
    Sign Up for Your First Event Pick one event from the list above and commit. Events are where the program comes alive — and where ribbons and friendships are made. Talk to your commander about registration and any fees.
