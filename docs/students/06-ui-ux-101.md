# 6. 🎨 UI/UX Design 101

Two fancy letters-pairs, one simple idea: **make things that look good and are easy
to use.**

- **UI = User Interface** = how it **looks** (colors, buttons, text, layout).
- **UX = User Experience** = how it **feels** to use (Is it easy? Confusing? Fun?).

> 🍔 **Restaurant analogy:** UI is how the food *looks* on the plate. UX is the whole
> dinner — was the menu easy to read? Did your food come fast? Were you happy?

You don't need to be an artist. You just need to follow a few rules that real
designers use. Let's go! 🚀

---

## Rule 1: Make it obvious 🔍

A good design tells people what to do *without* a manual.

- Buttons should **look** like buttons (so people know to click them).
- The most important thing should be the **biggest** thing.
- Link text should say where it goes: ✅ "Join the Cadets" not ❌ "Click here."

> Test: Show your page to someone for 5 seconds. Can they tell what it's for and
> what to do? If not, make it clearer.

---

## Rule 2: Use visual hierarchy 📏

**Hierarchy** means: the most important stuff stands out, less important stuff is
smaller. Guide the visitor's eyes like a path.

```
   BIG TITLE          ← see this first (most important)
   A short subtitle   ← see this second
   Normal paragraph text that explains more details...  ← read this if interested
   [ Button ]         ← the action you want them to take
```

Use **size**, **bold**, and **spacing** to show what matters most.

---

## Rule 3: Don't crowd things — use white space 🌬️

**White space** is the empty space around things. Beginners think empty space is
"wasted." It's not! It gives your design room to breathe and makes it easier to read.

- ❌ Everything jammed together = stressful and hard to read.
- ✅ Space between sections = calm and clear.

When in doubt, add a little more space. 🧘

---

## Rule 4: Be consistent 🔁

Pick a style and stick with it across the whole site.

- Use the **same colors** everywhere (e.g. our blue for all buttons).
- Use the **same font** for all body text.
- Make all your buttons look the **same**.

Consistency makes a site feel professional and trustworthy. Surprises confuse people.

---

## Rule 5: Colors & contrast 🌈

- Pick **2–3 colors** max. Too many colors = circus. 🎪
- Make sure text is **easy to read**: dark text on a light background, or light text
  on a dark background. **High contrast = readable.**
- ❌ Light gray text on a white background = nobody can read it.

> ♿ **Accessibility tip:** Some people can't see certain colors well. Good contrast
> helps *everyone*. Never use color *alone* to give info (e.g. don't make the only
> difference between "good" and "bad" red vs. green).

---

## Rule 6: Make it work on phones 📱

Most people will visit on their phone! Always check your design on a small screen.

- In your browser, press **F12** and click the little phone icon to preview a phone size.
- Make sure buttons are big enough to tap with a thumb.
- Make sure text doesn't run off the screen.

---

## Rule 7: Give feedback 💬

When someone does something, the site should *respond* so they know it worked.

- A button gets darker when you hover or tap it.
- After clicking "Send," show "✅ Message sent!"
- If something goes wrong, show a clear, friendly message (not a scary error code).

---

## ✏️ Design BEFORE you code: sketch it!

The pros don't start with code — they start with a **sketch** (a "wireframe").

1. Grab paper and a pencil. ✏️
2. Draw boxes for where things go (title here, button there, image here).
3. Don't worry about it being pretty — boxes and labels are perfect.
4. Show it to a friend. Does it make sense? Fix it on paper (way faster than in code!).

```
 ┌─────────────────────────────┐
 │        CADET CORPS          │  ← title
 │   Welcome! Join us today.   │  ← subtitle
 │        [  Join  ]           │  ← button
 │  ─────────────────────────  │
 │  📅 Events   👥 About  ✉️ Contact │  ← nav links
 └─────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## ✅ Before you call your design "done"

Run through the [UI/UX Checklist](./templates/ui-ux-checklist.md). Quick version:

- [ ] Is the most important thing the most obvious thing?
- [ ] Can I read all the text easily (good contrast)?
- [ ] Are the colors and fonts consistent?
- [ ] Is there enough space (not crowded)?
- [ ] Does it work on a phone screen?
- [ ] Do buttons look clickable and respond when used?
- [ ] Could a stranger figure out what to do without help?
